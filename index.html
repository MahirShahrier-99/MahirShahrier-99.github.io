Mahir Shahrier

Aspiring Environmental Researcher

Welcome to my professional corner of the web. I am an environmental researcher and engineer driven by a simple yet powerful goal: to leave the world cleaner, healthier, and more resilient for future generations. From an early age, I was fascinated by the natural world and dedicated myself to understanding how human activities shape and can protect our air, water, and communities. Today, I blend interdisciplinary approaches from AI Driven predictive analytics and geospatial mapping to advanced sensor design and policy development to address critical challenges in water management, air quality, climate extremes, and sustainable infrastructure. From particles in the air to patterns on the ground, my mission is to advance the Earth-system resilience and build eco-smart, climate-resilient solutions with data, empathy, and science. Whether I’m modeling climate extremes, optimizing waste management systems, developing nature-based solutions for pollution control, or collaborating with local leaders on scalable policies, my motivation remains constant: transform data and technology into practical solutions that empower people and safeguard ecosystems. I believe true sustainability emerges when science, innovation, and community engagement work hand in hand. That’s why I strive to make my research accessible, my tools intuitive, and my recommendations actionable. Ultimately, I seek to foster a world where environmental stewardship and human well-being reinforce one another, where resilient infrastructure coexists with thriving ecosystems, and where every choice is guided by equity, responsibility, and long-term vision. Thank you for visiting. Together, we can create a future in which the well-being of both people and the planet drives every decision.

Research

Why Water-Air-Waste-Energy Nexus research is a Key to a Sustainable Future..?

Environmental systems - water, air, waste, and energy - are deeply interconnected, yet we often treat them in isolation. Nexus research changes this by developing integrated solutions that address multiple challenges at once, like turning waste into energy while cleaning water and air. Real-world examples include refugee camps generating power from wastewater and smart cities using AI to manage resources efficiently. As climate change intensifies, this approach offers scalable, resilient solutions that bridge science and policy for global sustainability.

Thesis Presentation

This study analyzed road safety infrastructure on a 4-kilometer segment of Dhaka-Rajshahi Highway (N6) near Rajshahi universities using ViDA software and 53 variables. Road safety star ratings reveal pedestrians most vulnerable (65.61% unsafe segments), followed by bicyclists and motorcyclists. Infrastructure scored poorly with 2-1 star ratings. 46.34% segments show high injury risk requiring priority safety investments.

Presentation Link

Importance of Engineering Economics in Civil Engineering

This presentation covers Engineering Economics fundamentals for civil engineering applications. Engineering economics applies economic principles to help managers optimize decisions by minimizing costs and maximizing revenue. It involves seven core principles, cost analysis concepts, and capital investment decisions, exemplified by Bangladesh's major bridge projects.
